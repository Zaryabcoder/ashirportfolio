About Me

Revolution

A solution to automating and simplifying the manual task of conducting and managing drive tests surveys for network service providers.

Context

My last employer worked in the domain of telecom. I worked with them for just over a year where I got my hands dirty with the very niche field of telecom processes management and automation software. Revolution was one of the softwares I worked on. It aimed at automating and digitalizing the manual process of conducting and managing drive test surveys.

The Problem

Network service providers spend millions of dollars, hundreds of dedicated resources and incur huge costs in order to survey their network reception in a specific area. They buy very expensive equipment and have to hire many resources, train them, and set up a whole process on ground in order to gather data and KPIs to help improve their network.

Solution

Why not just make a mobile application that lets anyone travelling, commuting or moving from one point to other to gather the same data through their phones, in return of paid incentives? It would let telcos save millions in costs, resources and inventory to do so.

Research

Since I was absolutely new to the field of telecom, I first had to go through a lot of manuals and crash courses to get an idea of how telcos operate and provide their services. I had some experienced telecom engineers at my work that explained me the dynamics, job roles, processes, technicalities, and problems of such an organization. The research helped my get familiar with the technical aspects of such a process that helped me come up with a better solution to the problem at hand.

The Users

We identified two types of users for the solution:

The telecom vendor employee is the kind of user that uses the web based application to create drive test jobs, or "streams" in technical terms and manages them.

The other type of user is the commuter. They can use the mobile application that gathers the network data while they move, in return for a monetary incentive. To get started, and to keep an already complex solution simple, we identified the other types of telco employees to be the early adopters of this solution. We put the financial reward module at hold for the first iteration.

Challenges

After identifying user requirements, I worked on coming up with a suitable and user centric information architecture of both the web and mobile interfaces. For this phase, I used the knowledge gained through my research into the drive test survey process, conformed with the technical jargon in order to make it as streamlined for the web app users as possible. At the same time, I had to keep the process as simple for the commuter as possible, which was a challenge. After all this, we came up with web and mobile app wireframes for the telco vendor employee and commuter respectively.

Challenge:
I did not know a lot about the dynamics of the telecom industry, especially when it comes to the technical aspects, jargon, specific protocols and processes. Translating the complexity on one user's side into a simple interface for the other user type. There are two types of interfaces based on the user types. The telecom manager has a web based management module, whereas the commuter has a mobile app through which they conduct the jobs.

Solution:
I put at least two weeks getting myself familiarized with the niche field. I talked to telecom professionals, read manuals and especially the process I was tasked to "revolutionize". The research helped a lot with the task. The process included applying a design thinking based approach, translating requirements to simple use cases and prioritizing functionalities for the commuter. I targeted the top most use cases and hence hid the complexities from the commuter.
