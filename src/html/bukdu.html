About Me

Bukdu

An application for book readers struggling with making reading a habit and tracking their goals

Problem

How might we motivate busy people to read more consistently?

At the start of our program at DPS, we identified a problem common with most of the people who want to read books. They have a tough time following their reading goals and making it a habit.

Process

Understanding the user

We already had a broad target audience in mind: busy people who want to read more. To get more insight into the user, their motivations and pain points, we conducted interviews, surveys and made user personas. Based on the information gathered as a result, we took procrastinating students as our early adopters.

We identified the following two major pain points for the users:

  • Making reading a habit
  • Following reading goals



Research and Ideation

After collecting initial information on the users, we proceeded with further research methods to understand the user and ideate around their pain points to provide a suitable solution. We did interviews or surveys with around 100 users that provided us with some valuable insights.

For ideation, the team came up with methods and ways to address the pain points our potential users have. We used multiple techniques such as story boarding, worst-possible-idea and brainstorming.

Meanwhile, we also worked on our lean canvas that helped us get a better picture of the problem, solution, users and competitors. It aided us in considering the business aspect of the project as well.

After this, we created a story map that helped us identify our MVP. It was an important step as it gave us an initial picture of what features we would have to get started with.

Prototyping and testing

Based on the MVP, we got started with paper prototyping and wireframing, which would later on convert to hi fi mockups after validation from user testing.
